# Keystone — Knowledge Index

Frameworks Keystone internalized. Used as mental models when reading data and writing reports. Not quoted by name to the audience unless useful.

## Cash flow frameworks

**13-Week Cash Flow Forecast** (industry standard for trades)
- Rolling 13-week projection of cash in vs. cash out
- Cash in: AR collections, deposits from new contracts (GHL), expected loan draws
- Cash out: payroll (Ramp), AP scheduled, tax accruals, recurring (rent, insurance, software)
- Phase 2 weekly, Phase 3 monthly

**Profit First for Contractors** (Shawn Van Dyke)
- Allocate every revenue dollar to fixed buckets the moment it lands:
  - Profit (5-10%) | Owner's Pay (10-30%) | Tax (15-25%) | Operating (rest)
- Framing for budget discipline conversations with Josh + Matt

**Ellen Rohr cash-flow discipline**
- "Cash flow first, profit second" — a profitable contractor can still die from cash mismanagement
- Track weekly: cash balance, AR collected, AP paid
- Philosophical anchor for The Pulse

## Pricing & margin

**Markup vs. Margin** (Michael Stone, *Markup & Profit*)
- Markup is added on top of cost. Margin is the % of sale price that's gross profit.
- 50% markup = 33% margin. Most contractors confuse these and undercharge.
- Window installs target: 35-45% gross margin
- Roofing installs target: 25-35% gross margin

**20% Net Minimum** (Frank Blau doctrine)
- Below 20% net profit, you're not running a business — you're running a hobby
- Bright line for monthly P&L review

## KPIs

**Funnel-to-revenue** (Relentless-specific 70/35/15/1)
- 70 conversations → 35 booked appts → 15 sits → 1 close
- Validates revenue forecasts against setter activity

**Labor cost %** (residential trades)
- Total gross payroll / revenue
- Target: 25-30% for install businesses
- Above 35% → margin compression signal

**DSO — Days Sales Outstanding**
- Avg days from invoice send to cash collected
- Cash deals target: <7 days (deposit at signing)
- Financed / progress-billed target: <30 days

**WIP — Work in Progress**
- Value of jobs sold but not yet completed
- Healthy WIP > 2x monthly revenue = strong backlog
- Falling WIP = pipeline drying up before revenue feels it

**Cash conversion velocity**
- Avg days from contract signed → cash in Chase
- Target: same day for 50% deposit, <14 days for balance

## Job costing

**Job costing standard** (Markup & Profit)
- Every job has a budget at sale: materials, labor, sub, overhead
- Actuals tracked against budget mid-job (not after)
- Variance > 10% flagged before final invoice

**Crew-level margin tracking**
- Margin per crew per week — coaching opportunity when crew X runs 8%+ below crew Y
- Same logic for salesperson margin (discount discipline)

## Compliance calendar (Phase 2+)

**AZ TPT** — Transaction Privilege Tax, varies by city (Phoenix, Mesa, Tempe, Scottsdale all different). Monthly or quarterly based on volume.

**UT Sales Tax** — State + local. Typically monthly.

**Federal estimated tax** — Quarterly: Apr 15 / Jun 15 / Sep 15 / Jan 15.

**Form 941** — Quarterly payroll tax. End of month after quarter close.

**Form 940** — Annual FUTA. Jan 31.

**W-2 / 1099 deadlines** — Jan 31.

**AZ ROC license renewal** — Every 2 years.

**UT DOPL license renewal** — Annual.

**Workers comp audit** — Year-end true-up of payroll classifications.

**General liability + commercial auto** — Annual renewals.

## Industry benchmarks (the "are we healthy" comparisons)

### Residential window install (NAHB / Brady JKR data)
- Gross margin: 35-45%
- Net margin: 8-15%
- Labor: 18-25% of revenue
- Materials: 35-45%
- AOV target: $18,500
- Close rate target: 45%

### Residential roofing (NRCA / industry)
- Gross margin: 25-35%
- Net margin: 5-12%
- Labor: 15-20%
- Materials: 50-60%

### Home services (Tommy Mello / ServiceTitan benchmarks)
- Healthy net profit: 15-20%
- Scaling net profit: 8-15% (re-investing)
- Below 5% net = something is broken
